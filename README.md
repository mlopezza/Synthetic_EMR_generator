### Purpose and Overview
#### Business Problem

Medical records have always been a collaborative creation between patients and healthcare providers. The earliest known examples date back to Hippocrates in ancient Greece (around 400 B.C.), representing one of the first attempts to document clinical information in a structured way. Since then, records have evolved into electronic files—often hundreds of pages long and filled with terminology that patients struggle to understand.

In addition, concerns about patient data privacy make healthcare providers the custodians of sensitive information. While these strict privacy rules are necessary, they also limit access to healthcare data that could be extremely valuable in the era of data science. Privacy has become one of the biggest challenges when using health information for technological innovation. As a result, testing machine‑learning prototypes with real medical documents—without compromising anyone’s privacy—requires extensive administrative paperwork and advanced anonymization techniques, making early experimentation almost impossible.

On the other hand, building synthetic EMRs requires clinical expertise and significant time to structure a complete medical record. Synthetic EMR generation using LLMs, guided by a subject‑matter expert, can solve this problem during the initial stages of model testing, before applying the system to real medical documents after anonymization.

These synthetic EMRs were created through prompt engineering performed by a subject‑matter expert, combined with the capabilities of an LLM (Claude), to optimize time and effort.

##### Disclaimer
All documents are fully synthetic medical records generated by AI for software testing and demonstration purposes only. All patient data contained herein is fictional. Any resemblance to real persons, living or dead, is purely coincidental. Institutional names used in this document are fictional and do not represent real clinical documentation. This document must not be used for clinical, legal, or medical decision‑making purposes.

### Methodology
All EMRs were crafted based on Canadian clinical record models, specifically in accordance with the guidelines of the CPSO (College of Physicians and Surgeons of Ontario) and the CMPA (Canadian Medical Protective Association).

**Task and role** The task of the LLM will be to generate that document thinking as if it were a family physician, internist, or clinical doctor, and to write the complete medical record with all its parts:

1. **Cumulative Patient Profile (CPP)**
It is an executive summary that must always be at the beginning or easily accessible. It includes:

- **Identification:** Full name, date of birth, address, and provincial health card number (e.g., OHIP in Ontario, MCP in Newfoundland).
- **Current Medical Problems:** List of active diagnoses and long-term health conditions.
- **Medical and Surgical History:** Past surgeries, accidents, or serious illnesses.
- **Allergies and Adverse Reactions:** Critical documentation of medication or food allergies.
- **Current Medications:** Dose, frequency, and duration of current treatments.
- **Immunization History:** Detailed record of vaccines.
- **Family and Social History:** Relevant genetic diseases, occupation, and lifestyle habits.

2. **Encounter Notes**
Each time a patient visits the physician, the note should generally follow the SOAP format (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan) and contain:

- **Chief Complaint:** Patient’s main concern.
- **History of Present Illness:** Details of symptoms and their progression.
- **Physical Examination:**- Relevant findings, both positive and negative, and vital signs.
- **Diagnosis or Clinical Impression:** Definitive or differential diagnosis.
- **Management Plan:** Prescribed treatments, advice given, referrals to specialists, and follow-up plans.

3. **Supporting Documentation**

- **Investigation Reports:** Laboratory results, imaging reports (X-rays, MRI, etc.), and pathology.
- **Consultation and Discharge Reports:** Specialist notes and hospitalization summaries (discharge summaries).
- **Informed Consent:** Documentation of discussions about risks, benefits, and alternatives of treatments or procedures.
- **Communications:** Record of phone calls, emails, or relevant messages related to patient care.

### Second Correction to the output:
After the Second outcome were founded some renderization error that where corrected: 

Problem: Unicode superscript characters (⁹, ¹², ²) do not exist in the glyph map of the Helvetica font in ReportLab. When the renderer cannot find the glyph, it draws a black box instead.

Fix in emr_generator_v3.py: All Unicode superscripts were replaced with ReportLab’s native XML tag <super>...</super>, which works inside any Paragraph:

| Before (broken) | After (correct)        |
| --------------- | ---------------------- |
| ×10⁹/L          | x10<super>9</super>/L  |
| ×10¹²/L         | x10<super>12</super>/L |
| kg/m²           | kg/m<super>2</super>   |


This rule applies to any character not in the basic Helvetica set: never use Unicode superscript/subscript (⁰¹²³⁴⁵⁶⁷⁸⁹) in ReportLab — always use <super> or <sub> inside a Paragraph.
